Good Data Visualization
Title: U.S. Parents’ COVID‑19 Vaccine Attitudes (SurveyMonkey + Tableau)

Why it is good:

Effective visual encoding
The visualization uses bar charts to show how willing parents are to vaccinate their children. Bar length is a good way to represent numbers because it is easy to compare. This matches the idea from Cleveland and McGill that people judge length more accurately than area or color.

Filters and interaction
Viewers can filter the data by race, political views, and other categories. This makes it easier for users to explore patterns that matter to them, following Munzner’s idea of supporting both overview and detail.

Clear layout
The dashboard is clean and easy to read. It focuses on key information and avoids unnecessary decorations, which matches Tufte’s idea of keeping the data-ink ratio high.

Context and data source
The data source is shown clearly (SurveyMonkey and Boston Children’s Hospital), which builds trust. There is also context about when and how the data was collected.

Bad Data Visualization
Title: Honeycomb Pie Chart of Sugar and Water in Fruit

Why it is bad:

Misleading area representation
The chart uses hexagon shapes to show parts of a whole (like how much fructose or water is in the fruit). But the sizes of the shapes do not match the actual percentages. This makes it hard to see the real differences, which goes against the rules for good visual perception explained by Cleveland and McGill.

Hard to compare values
It is difficult to tell which item has the highest percentage. A bar chart would make it easier to compare.

Too much decoration
The honeycomb style looks fancy but adds no value. It distracts from the main message, which goes against Tufte’s advice to avoid chartjunk.

Missing labels and source
The chart does not include the data source or explain where the numbers come from. This makes it less trustworthy. Also, not all parts have percentage labels, making it harder to understand.

No helpful context
The chart does not explain why this data is important or what the user should learn from it.
